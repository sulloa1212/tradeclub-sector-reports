🛡️ Gamma Cushion (NDX): THIN / BORDERLINE

Think of gamma as a shock absorber. When it's "positive" (on), big options traders (dealers) automatically buy dips and sell rips — moves fade naturally. When it's "off" (negative), they do the opposite — weakness begets more weakness.

11How to Read This Report

Implied Move (1-SD %)
The options market's estimate of the "normal" move — 2-in-3 chance the actual move lands inside it. Think of it as the middle 67% of outcomes. It's a size estimate, not a direction call.
Two Horizons
Every index gets TWO reads: (1) the overnight gap — from tonight's close to tomorrow's open; and (2) a 1-week outlook — across the next ~3–5 trading sessions. Gap uses the 0.57 overnight factor; weekly uses the full trading variance.
Quiet Row (😴)
The first row in every odds table: the probability the move stays INSIDE the smallest threshold (≤0.5% gap / ≤1% weekly). It's the "nothing happens" scenario. All rows in the table add up to ~100%.
Risk Dial
Calm → Elevated → High → Extreme. Set by the implied move size vs the day's catalyst stack. Higher dial = wider expected swings — not "bad," just bigger potential moves in either direction.
Direction Split
The down % vs up % split in each row. Based on options put-skew — downside options cost more than upside ones, so the down tail is slightly fatter. The lean shows which tail is wider, not what will happen. Futures direction anchors the headline lean on a pre-open run.
Big Move Ranking
Ranks indices by probability of a weekly move exceeding 3% in either direction. Higher rank = larger expected swings. This is a SIZE ranking, not a prediction of direction.
The Cushion (Gamma)
Gamma is how much options dealers' hedging accelerates with price moves. When "positive/on," they buy dips and sell rips — moves fade. When "off/negative," they sell into weakness — moves snowball. The flip level (cushion line) is the price where the regime switches. Thin = near the flip.
Whole-Number Levels
Round numbers (SPX 50s, NDX 250s, RUT 25/50s, DJX 5s) where large blocks of options contracts cluster. These act as magnets — price is drawn toward them and often finds support/resistance there. Not magic; just where a lot of money is positioned.
Gap-Fill Tendency
Most small, ordinary gaps (≤0.5%) partially fill the same day — price tends to drift back toward the prior close. Momentum gaps (>1.5%, driven by strong earnings or macro) often don't fill quickly. The playbook tells you which type today's gap resembles.
Breadth Read
Compares how the four indices are moving relative to each other. NDX moving much more than DJX = tech-specific story (positioning-driven). All four moving together = genuine broad risk-on/risk-off. The breadth read tells you which type of move it is.
