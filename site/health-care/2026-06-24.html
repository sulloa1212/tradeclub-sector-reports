🏛️ Sector-Specific Policy Currents (Critical for Health Care)

  • Medicaid Cuts (OBBBA): The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" slashes Medicaid by ~$900B over a decade, with work requirements for ~20M enrollees starting 2027. CBO estimates ~10M will become uninsured. Managed care payers (UNH, ELV, CNC) face enrollment pressure; hospitals face rising uncompensated care. Direct headwind for managed care sub-sector.
  • MFN / Pharma Pricing: Trump administration "Most Favored Nation" pricing agreements link tariff relief to price reductions for Medicaid/cash-pay channels. Deals struck with 14 pharma companies, including LLY and Novo. Longer-term margin compression risk, but near-term deal certainty is preferred over tariff uncertainty.
  • Pharma Tariffs: Proposed 100% tariff on imported branded pharmaceuticals creates supply-chain risk, especially for generics. Most large-cap innovators have secured manufacturing agreements for relief, but headwinds persist for generics makers.
  • ACA Subsidy Expiry: ACA premium subsidies have expired (not renewed). Higher premiums + Medicaid disenrollment = rising uninsured population in 2026–27. Negative for managed care revenue; mixed for hospitals (more uncompensated care but also government funding relief programs).
  • Biotech M&A Surge: Deal flow is accelerating — LLY's $7.8B Centessa buyout closed June 24. ABT acquired Exact Sciences for $21B. Sector-wide M&A provides a floor for biotech valuations and a positive catalyst pulse for XBI/XLV.
