Bullish (oversold) IV High Cash-secured put • Bull put credit spread • Bull call debit spread When IV is high, options are expensive — you collect more premium selling puts. A bull put credit spread (sell a put, buy a lower put) is defined-risk and benefits from time decay AND a bounce.

RSI (Relative Strength Index, 0–100)
A momentum gauge showing how fast price moved vs history. Below 30 = oversold (sellers may be exhausted). Above 70 = overbought (buyers may be exhausted). Below 20 or above 80 = extreme. It's a starting point, not a buy/sell trigger alone.
Bollinger %B (0–1 scale)
Shows where price sits within its 20-day "normal range" (the Bollinger Bands). Above 1.0 = price is above the upper band (stretched). Below 0.0 = below the lower band (stretched). Best used with RSI to confirm.
Z-Score (how many standard deviations from mean)
Measures how far price is from its average in units of "normal" daily moves. A Z-score of ±2 means price is 2 standard deviations stretched — statistically unusual. The higher the absolute Z, the more stretched.
Composite Rank (★ stars)
★★★★ = stretched on 4+ measures; high-quality reversion thesis. ★★★ = stretched on 3 measures. ★★ = stretched on 2; needs more confirmation. ★ = 1 measure only; very low confidence; near-speculative.
IV (Implied Volatility)
How expensive options are right now. High IV = options cost more (premium sellers benefit). Low IV = options cost less (option buyers benefit). IV typically spikes during selloffs — which is why oversold + high IV is the ideal premium-selling setup.
Reference Mean
The target price the setup is reverting toward — usually the 50-day SMA (simple moving average). If the 50-day SMA is 10% above current price, that's the approximate upside target for the reversion thesis.

8How to Read This / Guardrails

The single most important rule: Reversion vs. Ruin.
Low RSI is necessary but NOT sufficient. A stock can be oversold because it's about to go to zero. Require ALL of these before treating a setup as valid:
  • ✅ The business is fundamentally intact (no fraud, no going-concern, no failed drug, no bankruptcy)
  • ✅ The move looks like sentiment/positioning excess — not a structural repricing
  • ✅ Stretched on 3+ measures (RSI, %B, distance from SMA, Z-score)
  • ✅ There is a well-defined "mean" to revert toward (e.g., 50-day SMA)
  • ❌ Reject: -50%+ in days with no stabilization; buyout/merger pin; debt blowup; accounting restatement
Special handling — Leveraged, Futures-Based & Vol ETFs:
  • 🔴 Long-Vol ETPs (VXX, UVXY, VIXY, UVIX): Trade ONLY when stretched UP (RSI/price spike). Never treat them as "oversold buys" — contango roll decay (~5%/month) grinds them lower permanently. A low RSI on UVXY is structural decay, not a setup.
  • 🟡 Inverse-Vol ETPs (SVXY, SVIX): Grind up in calm markets, crater on vol spikes. Their reversion setup is recovery after a volatility crash — the mirror of #1. Handle with extreme care (2018 Volmageddon: XIV collapsed 96% in one day).
  • 🟠 Leveraged / Futures ETFs (UCO, SCO, BOIL, KOLD, UNG, TQQQ, TBT): Volatility decay + roll cost means they deviate from any long-run mean. Use for short-term tactical reversion only (<1 week). For multi-week holds, prefer the unlevered fund or equity-sector proxy.
  • 📋 K-1 Tax Form: USO, UNG, UGA, DBC, UCO, BOIL issue a Schedule K-1, which complicates tax filing. For commodity exposure without K-1, prefer PDBC, BCI, or COMB where available.
  • ⚡ Proxy vs Spot: GDX, GDXJ, XOP, COPX, URA, MOO, SIL track companies not the commodity — they carry stock market beta and can diverge sharply from the underlying during market dislocations.
Verify before you trade — every time:
  1. Confirm current price and RSI on your own brokerage or charting platform (TradingView, TC2000, thinkorswim, etc.)
  2. Check the options chain: confirm strikes exist, bid-ask spread is tight, open interest is adequate
  3. Confirm the exact earnings date against your brokerage — dates shift; AI models can be wrong
  4. Size positions so a total loss is tolerable — defined-risk structures limit your maximum loss to the premium paid or spread width
  5. Paper-trade first, especially for any strategy new to you
