7How to Read This Report

This section explains the methodology — the "how we score" — so the body can focus on the results. Think of this as the key.

Direction Score (−100 to +100)
Weighted composite: 30% trend & price structure, 25% relative strength vs SPY (1–3 mo), 20% macro tailwind/headwind, 15% news & catalyst flow, 10% momentum/breadth (RSI, MACD). A +100 means maximum bullish alignment across all five inputs; −100 is maximum bearish. Zero means no clear edge.
Conviction (High / Medium / Low)
How confident we are in the score. High = 4–5 inputs aligned clearly. Medium = 2–3 inputs aligned with others neutral or conflicting. Low = marginal or contradictory signals — don't size up on Low-conviction calls.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)
Technically-led score on individual stocks: which name is most likely to move HARDEST in the sector's direction over 3 days–6 weeks. Inputs: 35% technical setup quality, 25% relative strength, 20% catalyst/news, 20% beta/range/liquidity. Higher = more likely to make a large directional move.
Fundamental Health Score (0–100)
Absolute business quality, independent of the chart. Inputs: 20% revenue growth, 20% EPS growth & beat history, 15% margins, 15% FCF & balance sheet, 10% analyst revisions, 10% guidance/backlog, 10% dividends/capital returns. This score tells you whether the company's fundamentals support or fight the technical direction.

Any stock with a confirmed earnings date within the next ~3 weeks is flagged with an amber ⚠. Earnings reports are binary events — the stock can move 5–20% in either direction regardless of trend.
