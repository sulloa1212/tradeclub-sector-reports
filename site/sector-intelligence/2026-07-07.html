6How to Read This Report

A quick reference for every score, label, and icon in this report.

Direction Score (−100 to +100)
A single number summarizing the sector's swing-trading tilt. +100 = maximally bullish (strong trend, positive macro, great news flow). −100 = maximally bearish. 0 = neutral, no edge.
Conviction (High / Med / Low)
How confident the analyst is in the direction call. High = multiple inputs agree. Low = conflicting signals. When conviction is Low, treat the score with appropriate skepticism and size down.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)
Applied to individual stocks. Measures the stock's potential to move hardest in the sector's direction over 3–6 weeks. Weighted: 35% technicals, 25% relative strength, 20% catalyst, 20% move-strength potential. Higher = more likely to lead the move.
Fundamental Health Score (0–100)
A separate, chart-independent score of business quality. Revenue growth, EPS growth & beat history, margins, FCF & balance sheet, analyst revisions, guidance, capital returns. Color-graded: Green ≥65 / Amber 45–64 / Red <45.
⛽ Fuel Tags
⛽ ADDS FUEL — Fundamentals confirm the direction. For longs: Health ≥65. For shorts: Health ≤40.
NEUTRAL — Fundamentals neither help nor hurt.
⚠ FIGHTS TREND — Fundamentals push against the direction (a healthy company is a riskier short).
⚠️ Earnings-in-Window Flag
When a stock has earnings during the 3–6 week swing horizon, it's flagged with ⚠️ INSIDE SWING WINDOW. This is event risk — the stock can gap ±10% on the print regardless of the setup. Reduce size and/or use defined-risk options strategies around earnings.
Direction Colors
Green = bullish / long thesis. Red = bearish / short-or-avoid. Amber = caution / event risk. Grey = neutral, no clear edge. A "big move" (high swing score) is not automatically good — it means more volatility in either direction.
How Scores Are Built
Sector Direction Score: Trend & price structure 30% + Relative strength vs SPY 25% + Macro tailwind/headwind 20% + News & catalyst flow 15% + Momentum/breadth 10%.
