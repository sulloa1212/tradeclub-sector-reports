7Options Structures Legend

This legend explains the generic method — what each structure is and when it maps to a setup. The actual results (which structures appear next to which names) are in the tables above.

RSI (14-day)
0–100 momentum gauge. Below 30 = oversold (potential bounce); above 70 = overbought (potential pullback). Extreme <20 / >80 = highest-conviction signal.
Bollinger %B (20,2)
Below 0 = price outside lower band (very oversold); above 1 = outside upper band (very overbought). Confirms RSI extremes.
Z-Score vs 50-day
How many standard deviations a price is from its 50-day average. Beyond ±2 = statistically stretched.
Implied Volatility (IV)
How expensive options are. IV elevated = options cost more → selling structures (put spreads, call spreads) collect richer premiums. IV low = buying structures (calls, debit spreads) cost less.
Oversold + High IV
→ Cash-secured put · Bull put (credit) spread · Bull call (debit) spread. All defined-risk first.
Oversold + Low IV
→ Long call · Bull call debit spread. Buying options is cheaper when IV is low.
Overbought + High IV
→ Call credit spread · Bear put (debit) spread. Selling calls collects rich premium; keep it defined-risk.
Overbought + Low IV
→ Long put · Bear put debit spread. Buying puts is cheaper when IV is low.
Defined-risk, always: Every structure above caps your maximum loss to the premium paid or the spread width minus credit received. Never sell naked options (uncapped loss). Always paper-trade any new structure before risking real money.

8How to Read This / Guardrails

Reversion vs. Ruin — the most important filter

A low RSI is necessary but not sufficient for a reversion trade. Every name on this report has been checked for:

  • Structural break? — Fraud, going-concern, failed drug trial, accounting restatement: REJECTED. None of today's names qualify.
  • Falling knife? — INTC is flagged as having real structural foundry risk that weakens (but doesn't eliminate) its reversion thesis. Size accordingly.
  • Buyout pin? — No M&A repricing situations identified today.
  • Sentiment excess over sound business? — MU/AMD/AVGO/SMH qualify. The TSM capex scare is new information but not a structural impairment of the businesses.
Special handling reminders
  • Long-vol ETPs (VXX, UVXY): Only trade when stretched UP (spike fade). Never "buy the dip" — structural contango decay grinds them lower over time.
  • Leveraged ETFs (TBT, UCO, BOIL, etc.): Use for short-term tactical reversion ONLY. Volatility decay + roll costs destroy value over weeks. Never hold for multi-week plays; prefer unlevered alternatives.
  • Equity proxies (GDX, XOP, OIH, XLE): These track companies, not the commodity. They carry equity beta and can diverge sharply from underlying spot prices.
  • K-1 issuers (USO, UNG, UGA, DBC, UCO, BOIL, CPER): These partnerships issue a Schedule K-1 at tax time, adding complexity. Prefer no-K-1 alternatives: PDBC (broad commodity), BNO (Brent crude), IAU (gold) where the exposure allows.
  • Earnings dates: Any setup marked ⚠ has an earnings date within ~30 days. A binary event can override any technical setup. Always verify dates against your brokerage before acting.
