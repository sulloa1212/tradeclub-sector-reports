How to Read This Report
Swing horizon
Everything is judged for a 3-day to 6-week hold — not day-trading, not long-term investing.
Direction score (−100 to +100)
A weighted blend of trend, relative strength, macro, news and momentum. Above 0 = net bullish, below 0 = net bearish; the further from zero, the stronger.
Conviction (High / Med / Low)
How aligned the inputs are. High = trend, macro and momentum all point the same way. Low = mixed signals.
Stock score (0–100)
"Conviction to move hardest in the sector's direction" — for longs, likely strongest gainers; for shorts, most-vulnerable names.
Fundamental Health (0–100)
A separate read on the underlying business — revenue & EPS growth, margins, FCF / balance sheet, analyst revisions, guidance.
⛽ Fuel indicator
Whether the fundamentals reinforce the trade direction. ⛽ Adds Fuel backs the move; ⛽ Neutral = a price bet; ⛽ Fights It = fundamentals cut against the trade.
Color language
Green = bullish / long · Red = bearish / short-or-avoid · Grey = neutral.
⚠ Earnings flag
If a company reports inside the swing window, that's event risk — a gap, not a clean technical move.
Technical levels
Approximate observations (entry zone / support / resistance), not guarantees. Re-verify live prices before acting.

All data is time-stamped ({{DATE}}) and goes stale quickly — prices, technicals and especially earnings dates should be re-verified before trading.
