EDUCATIONAL REFERENCE

Options Structures Legend

RSI (14) — Relative Strength Index Below 30 = oversold (selling exhaustion signal). Below 20 = extreme. Above 70 = overbought. Above 80 = extreme. RSI alone is NOT sufficient — combine with Bollinger %B, distance from MA, and Z-score.
Bollinger %B (20,2) Below 0 = price below the lower band (oversold extreme). Above 1 = price above upper band (overbought extreme). Best used in combination with RSI for confirmation.
Z-Score (price vs. 50-day mean) How many standard deviations price has moved from its 50-day average. Z > +2 or < –2 = statistically stretched. The higher the absolute Z-score, the more extreme the setup.
Distance from Moving Averages % above/below the 20-, 50-, and 200-day SMA. A name that is –15% below its 50-day on RSI < 30 AND below its Bollinger lower band = 3+ measure confirmation (high quality).
Oversold + High IV → Premium Selling (Bullish) Cash-Secured Put: sell a put below current price, collect premium, obligated to buy if assigned. Bull Call Spread: buy lower strike call, sell higher strike call — defined risk, defined reward. Put Credit Spread: sell OTM put, buy further OTM put — net credit, defined max loss.
Overbought + High IV → Premium Selling (Bearish) Call Credit Spread: sell OTM call, buy further OTM call — net credit, defined max loss. Bear Put Spread: buy ATM put, sell lower strike put — debit, defined risk. Both cap your maximum loss — always use these over naked short options.
Low IV → Option Buying When IV is low (relative to its own history), option premiums are cheap. Long calls (oversold setups) or long puts (overbought setups) become more attractive. Still define risk by using spreads.
Defined-Risk First — Always Every structure listed in this report uses defined maximum risk. Never sell naked calls or puts. Losses on naked short options can exceed your investment. Paper trade all strategies before risking real capital.

How to Read This Report / Guardrails

Reversion vs. Ruin Filter The most important filter in this framework

A low RSI is necessary but NOT sufficient. Before treating any oversold name as a reversion candidate, check:

  • REJECT Structural break: fraud, going-concern doubt, failed drug trial, bankruptcy, accounting restatement, dividend cut driven by financial distress.
  • REJECT –50%+ drop in days with no stabilization — likely more decline to come ("falling knife").
  • REJECT Buyout/merger repricing — stock is pinned to deal price, not reverting to a technical mean.
  • CAUTION Competitive structural gap (e.g., INTC foundry losses, AI accelerator lag) — partial falling-knife risk. Reduce size; demand more confirmations; use defined-risk structures only.
  • ✓ KEEP Sentiment/positioning excess over an otherwise fundamentally sound business, near prior support, with fundamentals intact (MSFT, NVDA, AMD, AVGO qualify).
Special Handling — Leveraged, Futures & Vol ETFs These do NOT revert to stable price means
  • Long-Vol (VXX, UVXY, VIXY): Only trade when stretched UP (spike). Never buy as "oversold" — contango decay grinds them lower permanently. The clean trade: fade the spike.
  • Inverse-Vol (SVXY, SVIX): Grind up in calm markets, crater on vol spikes. Their reversion setup is the recovery after a volatility crash — not a fade of their up-moves.
  • Leveraged ETFs (SQQQ, TQQQ, TBT, UCO, BOIL, etc.): Volatility decay + roll drag mean these deviate from any long-run mean. Short-term tactical only. Multi-week holds destroy capital via decay.
  • K-1 Tax Form: USO, UNG, UGA, DBC, UCO, BOIL, CPER, CORN, WEAT, SOYB issue Schedule K-1. Consult a tax advisor. Prefer PDBC, BCI, XLE, XOP for K-1-free alternatives where possible.
  • Proxy ETFs (GDX, GDXJ, URA, XOP, COPX, MOO, SIL): Track mining/energy companies, not the commodity spot price. Carry equity beta and can diverge significantly from the underlying commodity.
Verify Before Acting — The Non-Negotiables This report is a starting point, not a signal
  1. Confirm every price, RSI, moving average, and earnings date at your live brokerage before sizing a position. AI models can be wrong, and data can be stale by hours or days.
  2. Check options open interest and bid-ask spreads for the specific strike/expiration you intend to trade — thin spreads can make "paper profits" impossible to realize.
  3. Identify all events within 30 days (earnings, FOMC, economic data) that could be binary overrides. Reduce size or avoid the setup entirely if a major catalyst is imminent.
  4. Paper trade all new strategies first. If you haven't traded a specific structure (e.g., bull call spread) in a live account, paper trade it for at least one full cycle before risking real money.
  5. Size for the worst case. With defined-risk structures, the worst case is the full debit paid or the width of the spread minus credit received. Size so that losing the maximum on any single trade does not meaningfully impair your account.
  6. Quarter-end distortions. Today (Jun 29) and tomorrow (Jun 30) may see abnormal volume and flow patterns due to institutional rebalancing. Technical signals may be temporarily unreliable.
