7How to Read This Report

A quick guide to the scoring, labels, and meters used throughout — so you know what each number means before you act on it.

Direction Score (−100 to +100) Weighted across 5 inputs: trend/price structure (30%), relative strength vs SPY (25%), macro tailwind/headwind (20%), news & catalyst flow (15%), momentum/breadth (10%). +100 = maximum bullish; −100 = maximum bearish; 0 = no edge.
Conviction (High/Medium/Low) How confident we are in the directional score. High = multiple inputs aligned strongly. Low = one or two inputs driving the score; others are mixed or unclear. Low conviction = smaller position sizing.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100) Rates how strongly a specific stock is expected to move in its sector's direction over 3 days–6 weeks. Inputs: technical setup quality (35%), relative strength (25%), catalyst & earnings proximity (20%), beta/range/liquidity — "move-strength" (20%). This drives stock selection.
