6How to Read This Report

A quick reference guide — the generic method behind every score, badge, and meter in this report.

Direction Score (−100 to +100)
A single number summarizing how bullish or bearish each sector looks right now. The higher the positive number, the more bullish signals line up (trend, relative strength, macro, news, momentum). The more negative, the more bearish signals line up. Zero = neutral / no edge.

Weights used: Trend & price structure 30% · Relative strength vs SPY 25% · Macro tailwind 20% · News/catalyst 15% · Momentum/breadth 10%.
Conviction Level
How much we trust the Direction Score:
• High — multiple inputs agree; cleaner set-up
• Medium — directional lean, but some conflicting signals
• Low — mixed/choppy; score reflects a tilt, not a strong edge

When conviction is Low, the right move is often to wait for confirmation, not to size up.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)
A technically-led stock-level score. Not a measure of quality — a measure of how likely this stock is to move the hardest in the sector's direction over the 3-day-to-6-week swing horizon.

Inputs: Technical setup quality 35% · Relative strength 25% · Catalyst & news proximity 20% · Move-strength potential (beta, range, liquidity) 20%.
Fundamental Health Score (0–100)
Absolute business quality — independent of the chart. High = strong business; Low = weak. For longs, a high score adds fuel; for shorts-or-avoid, a high score fights the trade (healthy company = riskier short).

Inputs: Revenue growth 20% · EPS growth & beat history 20% · Margins 15% · FCF & balance sheet 15% · Analyst revisions 10% · Guidance/backlog 10% · Dividends/capital returns 10%.
⛽ Fuel Tag
A directional signal combining the Fundamental Health score with the trade direction:
• ⛽ ADDS FUEL — Fundamentals reinforce the trade (strong business for a long; weak business for a short)
• NEUTRAL — Fundamentals neither help nor hurt
• ⚠ FIGHTS TREND — Fundamentals work against the trade (a healthy company being shorted; a weak company being longed)

FIGHTS TREND doesn't mean the trade is wrong — it means be extra careful and use defined risk.
⚠ Earnings Inside the Window
When the next earnings date falls within our ~6-week swing horizon, the card shows an amber ⚠ warning. Earnings = a binary event. The stock can gap up or down 5–15% regardless of your analysis. Always:
1. Know the earnings date before entering
2. Size smaller than normal
3. Consider exiting before earnings, or using defined-risk options strategies

Never assume earnings will confirm your thesis.
