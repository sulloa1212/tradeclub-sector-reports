⚠️ SPX CUSHION (GAMMA): TRANSITIONAL — flip at $7,408

Here's what "cushion" means in plain English: options market-makers are forced to buy and sell the S&P 500 futures constantly to stay hedged. When they're in positive gamma (cushion ON), they buy when the market dips and sell when it rallies — like brakes on volatility. When they flip to negative gamma (cushion OFF), they do the opposite, pushing moves further in whatever direction they're already going.

The "cushion line" (gamma flip) for SPX is at $7,408 (source: FlashAlpha Jun 29 1:30 PM snapshot). SPX closed Monday at $7,440 — $32 above the flip, barely above. 11How To Read This Report

Definitions of every method and term used — how the numbers are made, not what they are for today (those are in the cards above).

Implied Move (1-SD)
The ±% range where options pricing says the market expects ~2-in-3 closes to land. Not a ceiling — about 1-in-3 moves will exceed it. Overnight gap formula: (vol_index/100) × √(1/252) × 0.57 × 100. 1-week formula: (vol_index/100) × √(5/252) × 100. The 0.57 factor reflects that overnight holds ~57% of full-session variance.
Two Horizons
Every index gets two risk reads: (1) the gap — the move from last close to Tuesday's open, anchored to overnight futures; and (2) the 1-week outlook — the expected range across the remaining trading sessions. The gap is the "now" read; the week is the "plan for the week" read.
Quiet Row (≤x_min)
The first row in every odds table — the chance the gap (or week) stays inside the smallest band (≤0.5% gap, ≤1% week). Every table's quiet row + all the threshold rows sum to 100%. This ensures the probabilities are coherent, not invented.
Risk Dial (Calm / Elevated / High / Extreme)
A 4-level gauge blending implied move size with fragility (is the cushion on or off? is a major catalyst in the window?). Gap anchors: <0.4% = Calm, 0.4–0.7% = Elevated, 0.7–1.05% = High, >1.05% = Extreme. Weekly anchors: <1.5% = Calm, 1.5–2.5% = Elevated, 2.5–3.5% = High, >3.5% = Extreme. A major catalyst inside the window bumps the dial up one notch.
Direction Split (Down % vs Up %)
The symmetric implied-move band is split between a downside and upside leg using put-skew (options markets almost always price downside gaps slightly higher — this is normal). The split shows which tail is fatter, not what will happen. On a pre-open run, the live futures sign overrides the base skew: if futures are up, the effective lean is upward regardless of base-rate skew.
Big Move Ranking
Ranks all four indices by P(|weekly move| > 3%), descending. This is a size ranking — it shows where the largest absolute swings are most likely to occur, in either direction. Being ranked #1 is not good or bad. The formula is 2 × (1 − Φ(3 / week_sigma)), where Φ is the standard normal CDF.
The Cushion (Dealer Gamma)
Options market-makers must constantly buy and sell futures to stay hedged. When they are long gamma (cushion ON): they buy dips and sell rallies automatically — damping moves. When they are short gamma (cushion OFF): they buy rallies and sell dips — amplifying moves. The "gamma flip" or "cushion line" is the price level where the regime switches. SPX cushion line today: $7,408. NDX: ~29,400–29,500 est.
Whole-Number Levels
Round-number price levels where large amounts of options open interest cluster: SPX (50s), NDX (250s), RUT (25/50s), DJX (5s). These act as magnets or barriers because options market-makers hedge more actively at these strikes. They are not magic — price breaks through them regularly — but they are the most common intraday turning points.
Gap-Fill Tendency
Roughly 70% of ordinary overnight gaps partially fill intraday (price revisits the opening gap before trending). Momentum gaps — large overnight moves on strong directional catalysts — are less likely to fill quickly. This report notes which type each gap resembles. "Fade it" = expect a partial fill; "respect it" = treat the gap level as support/resistance.
Breadth Read
The spread between how the four indices are moving IS a signal. If NDX moves sharply but RUT does not, the move is narrow (positioning/tech unwind — less durable). If all four move together with similar percentages, it's a broad risk-on or risk-off shift (more durable, harder to fade). This report flags which type is active and what would change the read.
