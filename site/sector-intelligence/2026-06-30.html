6How to Read This Report

Generic methodology — the "how" behind the numbers. The results are in sections 1–5 above.

Direction Score (−100 to +100)
A weighted composite score for each sector: 30% trend/price structure, 25% relative strength vs SPY, 20% macro tailwinds/headwinds, 15% news & catalyst flow, 10% momentum/breadth. Higher = more bullish the sector looks for a swing-trade horizon (3 days to 6 weeks). Zero = no clear edge. Not a prediction — an assessment of current conditions.
Conviction (High / Medium / Low)
How clean and consistent the evidence is across all 5 inputs. High = most inputs align clearly in one direction. Low = mixed signals or noisy tape. Low conviction = smaller position size or no trade at all.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)
Stock-level score weighted: 35% technical setup, 25% relative strength within sector, 20% catalyst/news, 20% move-strength potential (beta, range). Drives stock selection — which stocks are most likely to move hardest in the sector's direction. Higher = stronger setup, not a guarantee.
Fundamental Health Score (0–100)
Absolute business health, independent of the chart: 20% revenue growth, 20% EPS growth & beat history, 15% margins, 15% FCF & balance sheet, 10% analyst revisions, 10% guidance/backlog, 10% capital returns. This is separate from the swing score — a stock can have great fundamentals but a bad chart, or vice versa.
⛽ Fuel Tag (for Longs)
⛽ ADDS FUEL: Health ≥ 65 — fundamentals amplify the bullish chart read. NEUTRAL: Health 45–64 — neither adds nor fights the trend. ⚠ FIGHTS TREND: Health < 45 — poor business quality; the short may face an earnings surprise reversal. For shorts/avoids, the scale flips: Health ≤ 40 = ADDS FUEL (weak company = easier short); Health > 60 = FIGHTS TREND (healthy company is a riskier short).
⚠ Earnings In Window
If a stock reports earnings during the swing window (~3 days to 6 weeks), it is flagged with ⚠. Earnings create binary event risk — the stock can gap 5–20%+ in either direction regardless of your thesis. Experienced swing traders: size down before earnings or close the trade. Beginners: avoid holding through earnings unless you understand the risk fully.
Color Code: Green / Red / Amber / Grey
Green = bullish / long. Red = bearish / short-or-avoid. Amber = caution / watch. Grey = neutral / no clear edge. These are directional reads, not "good vs. bad" — a big move can be bearish for the stock but good for a short trade.
