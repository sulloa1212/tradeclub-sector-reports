7How to Read This Report

Direction Score (−100 … +100)

Weighted composite per sector: Trend & price structure (30%) + Relative strength vs. SPY (25%) + Macro tailwind/headwind (20%) + News & catalyst flow (15%) + Momentum/breadth (10%). A score of +70 means 70% of maximum possible bullish evidence is present — not a certainty, a probability-weighted lean.

Conviction: High / Medium / Low

How clean and aligned the evidence is. High = multiple confirming signals, clear setup. Medium = mixed evidence or one key variable (like tonight's earnings) still unresolved. Low = directional lean exists but too noisy for high-confidence swing entry.

Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)

Technically led score for individual stocks within a drilled sector — driven by chart setup (35%), relative strength (25%), catalyst/earnings proximity (20%), and move-strength potential (20%). Higher = this stock is more likely to move hardest in the sector's direction over 3 days to 6 weeks. This drives stock selection, not fundamental quality.

Fundamental Health Score (0–100)

Independent business-health score: revenue growth (20%), EPS growth & beat history (20%), margins (15%), FCF & balance sheet (15%), analyst revisions (10%), guidance/backlog (10%), capital returns (10%). Separate from the chart — a weak business can still have a strong technical trade, and vice versa.

⛽ Fuel Tags

Longs: Health ≥65 → ⛽ ADDS FUEL (business backs the chart) · 45–64 → NEUTRAL · <45 → ⚠ FIGHTS TREND
Shorts/Avoid: Health ≤40 → ⛽ ADDS FUEL (weak business supports short) · 41–60 → NEUTRAL · >60 → ⚠ FIGHTS TREND (healthy business = riskier short).

⚠ Earnings-in-Window

Any stock with confirmed earnings within the current swing horizon (roughly next 6 weeks) is flagged. Earnings inside the window = binary event risk — the stock can gap ±10–30% overnight on results.
