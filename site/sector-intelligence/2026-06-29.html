How to Read This Report

Direction Score (−100 to +100) A weighted composite of 5 inputs: ETF trend (30%), relative strength vs SPY (25%), macro tailwind/headwind (20%), news & catalyst flow (15%), momentum/breadth (10%). Scores reflect the swing-trading outlook (3 days–6 weeks), not a long-term view.
Conviction (High / Med / Low) How clean and well-supported the directional signal is. High = multiple inputs agree, data is fresh and clear. Low = noisy, conflicting, or thin data. Never trade Low-conviction setups at full size.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100) A technically-led score (35% technicals, 25% relative strength, 20% catalyst/earnings proximity, 20% move-strength potential) that ranks stocks by their likelihood of producing the STRONGEST move in the sector's direction. This is what drives stock SELECTION within a sector — not the quality of the business.
Fundamental Health (0–100) A separate, business-quality score: revenue growth (20%), EPS growth & beat history (20%), margins (15%), FCF & balance sheet (15%), analyst revisions (10%), guidance/backlog (10%), dividend/capital returns (10%). Completely independent of the chart. A 65+ is a healthy company; sub-45 is challenged.
⛽ Fuel Tags For LONGS: Health ≥65 = ⛽ ADDS FUEL (fundamentals support the swing); 45–64 = NEUTRAL; <45 = ⚠ FIGHTS TREND (weak business fighting upward gravity).
For SHORTS/AVOID: Health ≤40 = ⛽ ADDS FUEL (weak business supports the bear case); 41–60 = NEUTRAL; >60 = ⚠ FIGHTS TREND (shorting a healthy company is dangerous).
⚠ Earnings-in-Window If a stock's next earnings date falls within the approximate 3-day to 6-week swing horizon, it is flagged amber (⚠). This is both a catalyst AND a binary risk event. You may choose to trade INTO the earnings catalyst or EXIT before to avoid the binary gap risk — decide before entering.
Dials: CALM / ELEVATED / HIGH / EXTREME Calibration for sector gauges: CALM = mild directional signal, limited action; ELEVATED = moderate, worth watching; HIGH = strong signal, actionable; EXTREME = maximum signal strength but also maximum risk of mean-reversion.
