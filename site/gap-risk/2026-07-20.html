🛡️ What “the cushion” means here (gamma, plain English) Gamma exposure — how much dealers must hedge as price moves — is currently negative on NDX. That means market-makers are short gamma: when the index falls, they must sell more to stay hedged, pushing it lower; when it rallies, they must buy, pushing it higher. There is no natural cushion from dealer flows. The gamma-flip level near 29,595–29,611 is where that behavior would reverse — but with 60 pointing modestly lower and spot roughly 750 points below the flip, crossing back above it in a single overnight session is unlikely without a major positive catalyst. Treat every move as potentially self-reinforcing until proven otherwise.

Pre-market: overnight futures already trading — the direction read is sharpest. Mid-session / post-market: the direction read is driven by the drift signal (short-term trend + today’s tape + gamma) plus skew; it sharpens as overnight futures trade.
Overnight gap
This session’s close → the next session’s open (~1 closed night), so the implied band is the plain overnight 1SD.
1-Week implied move
The one-standard-deviation band over the next ~5 trading sessions, from each index’s own vol index (full-session variance, no weekend bump). A size, not a direction.
Odds bands
Each row is a slice of where the open could land and the odds it lands in that slice. The worst lvl is the far (outer) edge of the slice; the near edge is the row above it. The slices don’t overlap, so the odds add up — all down bands sum to the down lean, all up bands to the up lean, everything to 100%. For a level between the marks (like your actual breakeven), use the Breakeven Calculator.
Direction Split
The band sliced into a down leg and an up leg by a model with two inputs: a directional drift (overnight futures + short-term trend + gamma regime) that sets which way it leans, and a downside skew that keeps the down tail fatter. The legs sum back to the band total. It’s a modest, conditional lean — not a forecast of what will happen.
Big Move Ranking with Probabilities
The four indices ranked by the options-implied chance of a >3% move (either direction) over the next ~5 sessions. A size ranking — where the widest swings are most likely, not which way.
Breakeven Calculator
Enter your trade’s two breakevens and it returns the odds the index stays between them (profit) vs gaps out either side, for the overnight or 1-week horizon. Uses the same drift+skew model, evaluated at your exact levels. Runs entirely in your browser on the numbers baked in at generation — it’s a snapshot, so re-check against live prices.
Risk dials
Calm / Elevated / High / Extreme — blend implied size with fragility (cushion/gamma and catalysts). Each index has an overnight dial and a 1-week dial.
The Cushion (gamma)
Positive = dealers buy dips/sell rips, moves fade. Off (below the “cushion line”) = they sell into weakness, moves snowball. Thin = sitting right at the line.
Whole-number levels
Round numbers act as magnets (option open-interest clusters there). Approximate — re-verify live.
Breadth read
The spread between the four indices is a signal: a narrow tech move is positioning; a broad one is real risk-on/off.
