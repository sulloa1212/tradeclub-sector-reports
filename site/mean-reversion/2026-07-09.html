7Options Structures Legend

This legend explains how the methodology maps direction + IV to a structure type. The body of the report gives you the specific ticker results. All structures should be paper-traded first — never risk real money on a new structure you haven't practiced.

RSI (Relative Strength Index) A 0–100 momentum gauge. Below 30 = selling may be exhausted (oversold); above 70 = buying may be exhausted (overbought). Extreme = below 20 / above 80. RSI alone is NOT enough — combine with other measures.
Bollinger %B (Price vs Band) Measures where price sits relative to a 20-day volatility band. Below 0 = price outside the lower band (very stretched down); above 1 = price outside the upper band (very stretched up).
Z-Score (Standard Deviations) How far price is from its average, measured in standard deviations. Z-score of −2 or lower = very stretched below average; +2 or higher = very stretched above average.
% from 50/200-day SMA How far price is from its 50-day or 200-day simple moving average (the "mean"). A name far below its average is a reversion candidate; far above suggests potential pullback.
IV (Implied Volatility) How expensive options are right now. High IV = options cost more = favor SELLING premium (you collect more). Low IV = options cost less = favor BUYING options.
Oversold + High IV → Premium Selling Direction: bullish (expecting bounce). High IV = sell premium. Structures: Cash-secured put (sell a put, hold cash to buy shares if assigned) · Put credit spread (sell a put, buy a lower put — limits max loss).
Oversold + Low IV → Premium Buying Direction: bullish. Low IV = cheaper to buy options. Structures: Bull call spread (buy a call, sell a higher call — caps profit but limits cost) · Long call (simple, but costs more in low-IV environments too).
Overbought + High IV → Call Credit Spread Direction: bearish (expecting pullback). Sell a call at/above current price, buy a higher call — collects premium, limits loss if wrong. High IV means better premium collected.
Overbought + Low IV → Bear Put Spread Direction: bearish. Buy a put, sell a lower put — costs a debit but limits max loss to that debit. Cleaner in lower-IV environments.
Iron Condor Sell both a call spread AND a put spread around an expected range. Profits if price stays between strikes. Best when IV is elevated and you expect a rangebound move (e.g., post-earnings mean reversion).
Composite Stretch Score Names ranked by how many indicators (RSI, %B, Z-score, distance from mean, consecutive days) confirm the extreme. 3+ confirmations = higher quality setup vs. RSI alone. The more confirmations, the higher on the list.
Vol ETP Special Rule VXX/UVXY: only fade when stretched UP (RSI/price spike). Never "buy the dip" in VXX/UVXY — contango decay makes low RSI a drift, not a setup. Leveraged ETFs (TQQQ, UCO, BOIL): short-term tactical only — decay prevents multi-week holds.

8How to Read / Guardrails

The most important rule in mean-reversion trading: a low RSI is necessary but not sufficient. Here is the filter that separates a reversion setup from a "falling knife."

🗡 Reversion vs. Ruin Filter — REJECT these patterns:
❌ Structural break: fraud, going-concern doubt, dividend cut, failed drug trial, bankruptcy, debt blowup, accounting restatement — the stock is cheap for a reason.
❌ Drop of 50%+ in days with no stabilization — likely more pain to come.
❌ Buyout/merger repricing — the stock is now pinned to a deal price, not a mean.
✅ KEEP: Sentiment/positioning excess over an otherwise sound business, ideally near a prior support level.
🔍 How to confirm a reversion setup:
• Price begins to stabilize after the drop (higher lows on small timeframe)
• RSI starts to curl back above 30 (confirmation, not the trigger)
• Volume: larger on up-days than down-days after the flush
• Near a prior support level (chart context matters)
• 3+ technical measures confirm the extreme (not just RSI alone)
⚠ Leveraged & Futures-Based ETFs — Special Rules:
UCO, BOIL, KOLD, TQQQ, SQQQ, TBT, and all 2x/3x products: short-term tactical only. Volatility decay + futures roll costs mean they deviate from any long-run mean — do NOT hold for multi-week reversion trades. For energy reversion, prefer XLE or XOP (equity proxies). For bond reversion, prefer TLT or IEF over TBT.
⚠ K-1 Tax Forms:
USO, UNG, UGA, DBC, UCO, BOIL, CPER, and most commodity futures ETFs issue a Schedule K-1 at tax time (more complex than a standard 1099). Check with your tax advisor. Prefer no-K-1 alternatives where available: PDBC, BCI, or COMB for broad commodities; XLE/XOP for energy equity exposure.
⚠ Proxy vs. Spot — Know What You Own:
