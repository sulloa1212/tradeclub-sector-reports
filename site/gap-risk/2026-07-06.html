BORDERLINE / NEAR FLIP (est.)

11How To Read This Report

Definitions of every term used above — start here if anything was unclear.

Implied Move (1-SD)
The options-market's best estimate of how much an index is likely to move. "1-SD" means one standard deviation — roughly a 2-in-3 chance the actual move lands inside this band. It measures SIZE, not direction. The formula uses the relevant volatility index (VIX, VXN, etc.) and time.
Two Horizons
Gap horizon: the move from Thursday's close to Monday's open (the weekend gap). Week horizon: the expected range over the full 5-session week (Jul 6–10). Different formulas; the weekly uses full-session variance, no overnight adjustment.
Quiet Row (≤0.5% gap / ≤1% week)
The first row in every odds table — the chance the move stays SMALL (inside the smallest threshold). It ensures every row in the table adds up to ~100% total. A "quiet" open doesn't mean boring — it just means no big directional gap.
Risk Dial
A four-level rating of how big the expected gap or weekly move is: Calm = small, Elevated = moderate, High = significant, Extreme = unusually large. Based on the implied-move size plus any catalyst or cushion adjustment. A "High" dial means bigger-than-average swings, not necessarily a bad day.
Direction Split (Down % / Up %)
How the symmetric probability band is split between down and up moves. The split uses "put-skew" — options traders almost always pay more for downside protection, which tilts the tail slightly toward down. On a pre-open run, the actual futures direction anchors the lean. The split shows which tail is FATTER, not what will happen.
Big Move Ranking
Ranks all four indices by P(|weekly move| > 3%) — the probability of a big swing in either direction. This is a SIZE ranking only. #1 = widest expected swings, not the best or worst direction.
The Cushion (Dealer Gamma / Gamma Flip)
Options market-makers ("dealers") hold large positions and hedge them mechanically. When their "gamma" (rate of change in their hedge) is positive (cushion ON), they buy dips and sell rips — the market's shock absorber is working. When gamma flips negative (cushion OFF), they sell into weakness, amplifying moves. The exact price where this flips is the "cushion line" or "gamma flip." Estimates are marked "est." — always verify with live dealer-positioning data.
Whole-Number Levels
Round price levels (SPX 50s, NDX 250s, RUT 25/50s, DJX 5s) where large options open interest clusters. These act like magnets — price often stalls or bounces near them. Also includes the 1-SD gap/week ranges and the estimated cushion line.
Gap-Fill Tendency
Most ordinary gaps (≤0.5%) partially fill intraday as the market drifts back toward the prior close. Momentum gaps (driven by real news/catalysts) often don't fill and instead continue in the gap direction. This report notes which is more likely for each index.
Breadth Read
Comparing the four indices' moves to each other is itself a signal. If only NDX moves while RUT/DJX are flat, it's a tech-positioning unwind, not real risk-on/off. If all four move together, it's a genuine macro/sentiment shift. This report calls which type is occurring and what would change it.
VXN / VIX Ratio
VXN is the volatility index for NDX; VIX is for SPX. When VXN/VIX > 1.3, options traders are specifically worried about tech (NDX) more than the broad market. When the ratio is near 1.0, stress is uniform. Currently ~1.28 (est.) = mild tech-specific concern.
