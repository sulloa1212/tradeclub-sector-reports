6Volatility Callout

🟢 VOL NOT STRETCHED UP — No Spike-Fade Setup Today

VXX and UVXY are not at stretched upside extremes as of this pre-open run. The vol spike that accompanied the Iran-war peak (Q1 2026) has largely unwound. No spike-fade trade is present today.

How to think about vol ETPs:

  • Long-vol (VXX, UVXY) — UPSIDE fades only. These revert DOWN reliably after a VIX/fear spike, because both suffer from contango — VIX futures usually trade above spot, so the daily roll costs money; holding VXX or UVXY for weeks erodes capital. A low RSI on VXX/UVXY is structural decay, not a buy.
  • When would a fade setup appear? If VXX/UVXY RSI spikes above 75–80 on a sudden fear event (new Hormuz escalation, macro shock, large tech earnings miss), that is the highest-confidence fade in this framework — the spike reverts as fear fades. Watch for it.
  • ⚠ SVXY (inverse vol) grinds UP in calm markets but craters on vol spikes. SVXY profits when VIX futures decline, which is most days in calm markets — it delivers steady gains, then sharp drawdowns when volatility spikes. Do not treat its up-move as overbought. Its reversion setup is a recovery after a vol crash.

7Options Structures Legend

This legend explains the method — what each term means and how direction + IV level maps to an educational structure. The actual results (which names to watch) are in the tables above.

RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A 0–100 momentum gauge. Below 30 = oversold (selling may be exhausted). Above 70 = overbought (buying may be exhausted). Below 20 / above 80 = extreme. RSI alone is not enough — always confirm on 3+ measures.
Bollinger %B (0,2)
Measures where price sits within its Bollinger Band (20-day, 2 standard deviations). Below 0 = price outside lower band (oversold territory). Above 1 = price above upper band (overbought). Confirms RSI extremes.
Z-Score vs 50-day Mean
How many standard deviations price is from its 50-day average. Z above +2 or below −2 is statistically stretched and historically reverts. Used alongside RSI/Bollinger for a composite read.
% From 50d / 200d SMA
How far current price is from the 50-day or 200-day simple moving average. These are the "means" the price can revert toward. A name −20% below 50d AND oversold RSI AND Bollinger %B <0 is extreme on 3 measures.
IV (Implied Volatility)
The market's expected future move, priced into options. High IV = expensive options = favor selling strategies (credit spreads). Low IV = cheap options = favor buying strategies (debit spreads, long calls/puts). Check IV Rank (IVR) vs historical before sizing.
Bull Call Spread (defined-risk long)
Buy a lower-strike call, sell a higher-strike call. Limits both profit and loss. Best when IV is low-moderate and you want upside exposure without large premium outlay. Used for oversold + low IV setups.
Put Credit Spread (defined-risk bullish)
Sell a higher-strike put, buy a lower-strike put. Collect premium; profit if price stays above the sold strike. Best when IV is elevated (you receive more premium) and you expect a floor to hold. Used for oversold + high IV.
Bear Put Spread (defined-risk short)
Buy a higher-strike put, sell a lower-strike put. Profits when price falls. Limited risk and limited reward. Best for overbought setups where you expect a pullback but not a crash.
Call Credit Spread (defined-risk bearish)
Sell a lower-strike call, buy a higher-strike call. Collect premium; profit if price stays below the sold strike. Best for overbought + high IV. Used when a name has run too far and IV is juicy.
Direction + IV → Structure Map
Oversold + High IV → Put credit spread / cash-secured put (collect elevated premium while betting on a floor). Oversold + Low IV → Bull call spread (cheap debit, upside reversion). Overbought + High IV → Call credit spread / bear put spread. Overbought + Low IV → Bear put spread (cheap downside protection). Always defined-risk first.

8How to Read This Scan / Guardrails

Start here if you are new: A stretched RSI is a starting point for research, not a trade signal. The scan tells you WHERE to look, not WHEN to act. Always verify every data point against your own brokerage before committing real money. Paper-trade first.

The Reversion-vs-Ruin Filter

We reject a name if ANY of these are true:

  • Structural break: Fraud, going-concern doubt, dividend cut, failed drug trial, bankruptcy, accounting restatement. A low RSI on a broken company is a falling knife, not a setup.
  • Enormous, unstabilized drop (−50%+ in days with no support). Likely more to come.
  • Buyout/merger pin: The stock is anchored to a deal price, not its historical mean.

We keep a name if: The move looks like sentiment/positioning excess over an otherwise sound business, ideally near a prior support/resistance level where buyers previously stepped in.

Proxy, K-1, and Leverage Caveats

  • Proxy ETFs (GDX, GDXJ, XOP, OIH, URA, COPX): These track companies, not the commodity. They carry equity beta — they can diverge from the underlying metal or commodity. A miners ETF is NOT a gold ETF.
  • K-1 ETFs (USO, UNG, UGA, DBC, UCO, BOIL, CPER, some CurrencyShares): Issue a Schedule K-1 at tax time, which complicates tax filing. Prefer K-1-free alternatives (PDBC, XLE/XOP for energy) where the exposure allows.
  • Leveraged / futures ETFs (SOXL, TQQQ, UCO, BOIL, TMF, TBT): They track VIX futures or leveraged benchmarks; persistent decay, imperfect correlation, annual losses of 60–80% for long vol products. Volatility decay + contango/backwardation mean they drift away from any long-run mean. Use only for SHORT-TERM tactical trades; for multi-week holds prefer the unlevered fund or equity-sector proxy.
  • Long-vol ETPs (VXX, UVXY): Only trade UPSIDE spikes — never buy because RSI is low. Low RSI is roll decay, not a setup. The clean trade is fading the spike.

Verify Before Trading — Every Time

  • Confirm current price, RSI, and options chain in your live brokerage — not from this report.
  • Check earnings / event dates within 30 days before sizing any position.
  • Confirm options open interest and bid/ask spread — thin chains mean wide spreads that eat your edge.
  • Never use more than you are willing to lose entirely on a single defined-risk spread.
  • Paper-trade every strategy before using real money.
