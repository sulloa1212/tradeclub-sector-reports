7Options Structures Legend

This section explains what each educational structure means — the why you'd use it and the max risk. Always paper-trade first.

OVERSOLD + HIGH IV
Cash-Secured Put
Sell a put at a strike below current price. You collect premium and are obligated to buy 100 shares if the stock falls to your strike. Max loss = strike price × 100 (capped). Best when you're willing to own the stock at a lower price. IV is elevated → you collect more premium.
OVERSOLD + HIGH IV
Put Credit Spread
Sell a put at a higher strike, buy a cheaper put at a lower strike — same expiry. You keep the net credit if the stock stays above your sold strike. Max loss = width of the strikes minus premium received. Defined risk — your worst case is capped.
OVERSOLD + LOW/MOD IV
Bull Call Spread
Buy a call at a lower strike, sell a call at a higher strike. Net cost = debit (you pay upfront). Max loss = premium paid. Max gain = width of the strikes minus premium paid. Use when you want directional upside without paying for unlimited upside you don't expect.
OVERBOUGHT + HIGH IV
Call Credit Spread
Sell a call above current price, buy a higher call for protection. You keep the net credit if the stock stays below your sold strike. Max loss = width minus premium. Defined risk — protects you if the stock keeps running.
OVERBOUGHT + MOD IV
Bear Put Spread
Buy a put at a higher strike, sell a cheaper put at a lower strike. Net debit (you pay upfront). Max loss = premium paid. Max gain = width minus premium. Use when you expect a pullback but want to limit your cost vs. buying a naked put.
KEY RULE — ALL STRUCTURES
Defined Risk First, Always
Naked options (uncapped risk) are NOT discussed here. Every structure above has a defined maximum loss known before you enter the trade. That is the educational standard for this report. Paper-trade first. Confirm data before acting.

Technical Indicator Quick Reference

RSI (14-day)
A 0–100 momentum gauge. Below 30 = oversold (sold too far, too fast). Above 70 = overbought. Below 20 or above 80 = extreme. It is NOT a guarantee of reversal — stocks can stay extreme.
Bollinger %B
Measures price position within Bollinger Bands (a price envelope). Below 0 = price is outside the lower band (very stretched low). Above 1 = outside the upper band (stretched high).
Z-Score vs 50-day SMA
How many standard deviations (units of normal variation) a price is above or below its 50-day average. Z > +2 or < –2 = statistically unusual. High Z-score = stretched further than normal.
% vs 50/200-day SMA
Simple distance — how far is the current price from its recent average. –20% below the 50-day = significantly weaker than recent history. Useful context alongside RSI.
Williams %R
Similar to RSI but inverted. Near –100 = oversold. Near 0 = overbought. Confirms RSI signals when both read the same direction.
IV (Implied Volatility)
How expensive options are — driven by expected future moves. Elevated IV → options are pricey → favor selling premium (credit spreads, cash-secured puts). Low IV → options are cheap → favor buying (debit spreads, long calls/puts).

⚡ Leverage Warning
