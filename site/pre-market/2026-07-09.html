How to Read This Report
Pre-Market edition
This report is built from overnight data and covers what you need to know before the 9:30 AM ET open. Prices move — re-verify live before any trade.
Bias / Confidence
Overall market bias (Bull / Bear / Neutral) and a conviction score 1–10. This is a synthesis of all data in the packet — not a single-indicator call.
Direction score (−100 to +100)
A weighted blend of 1-day and 1-month sector performance. Above 0 = net bullish, below 0 = net bearish. Sectors ranked best-to-worst.
IV Percentile / IV Rank
Where a stock's implied volatility (the market's expected move price) sits relative to its own history. High %ile = expensive options; low %ile = cheap options.
Vol/OI Ratio
Options volume divided by open interest (existing contracts). A ratio above 1.0 means more contracts traded today than currently exist — fresh new positioning. This is the "freshness" signal.
Sweep
An options order that simultaneously hits multiple exchanges to get filled quickly. It signals urgency — someone needs to get in fast and doesn't care about getting the best price.
GEX (Gamma Exposure)
How dealers (market makers — the people who take the other side of your options trades) are positioned. When dealers are "short gamma," moves amplify. When "long gamma," moves dampen. It's the invisible hand behind options-driven price action.
Dark Pool
Off-exchange institutional trading. Large blocks printed at the ask (offer price) generally signal buyers — someone willing to pay up to accumulate.
Color language
Green = bullish / long · Red = bearish / short-or-avoid · Amber = caution / watch · Grey = neutral.
