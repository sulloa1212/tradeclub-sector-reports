Educational reference — how the setups map to structures

7Options Structures Legend

Use this table as a quick reference for why each directional/IV combination suggests a particular educational structure. These are frameworks — always confirm liquidity, spreads, and expiration before trading. Paper-trade first.

Oversold + High IV
Best for: Cash-Secured Put / Bull Put Credit Spread. You collect elevated premium. If the stock bounces (your thesis), the put expires worthless. If it keeps falling, you're assigned at a net lower effective cost. Defined-risk via spreads.
Oversold + Low IV
Best for: Bull Call Spread / Long Call. Options are cheap; you buy the right to profit from the bounce without overpaying. Risk is the premium paid. Low IV = cheaper options buying.
Overbought + High IV
Best for: Call Credit Spread / Bear Put Spread. Sell expensive call premium above resistance. Max profit if the stock pulls back or stays flat. The spread caps your risk vs naked call-selling.
Overbought + Low IV
Best for: Bear Put Spread / Long Put. Buy puts while they're relatively cheap. Risk = premium paid. Works if the pullback materializes before IV expands further.
RSI (0–100 gauge)
Relative Strength Index: measures recent up-days vs down-days over 14 periods. Below 30 = oversold (too many down days in a row). Above 70 = overbought. Extreme: <20 or >80. Never trade RSI alone — confirm with price levels and other indicators.
Bollinger %B
Where price sits within its 20-day Bollinger Bands. <0 = price below lower band (oversold stretch). >1 = price above upper band (overbought stretch). Complements RSI — a name outside the band AND with extreme RSI is doubly stretched.
Z-Score
How many standard deviations (σ, a unit of typical daily variation) price is from its 50-day average. Z > +2.0 = overbought stretch; Z < –2.0 = oversold stretch. More extreme than RSI alone; a Z-score > ±2.5 is very rare.
Implied Volatility (IV)
What the options market is pricing as expected future volatility (expressed as %). High IV = expensive options → favor selling premium (credit spreads, cash-secured puts). Low IV = cheap options → favor buying (debit spreads, long calls/puts). Always compare IV to the stock's own history (IV Rank/Percentile).
Defined-Risk First
Every structure in this report is defined-risk (spreads, not naked options). A credit spread means you collect money upfront and define your max loss. A debit spread means you pay upfront and define your max loss. Both are safer starting points for new traders than naked options or leveraged futures.
Filters & caveats — read before trading anything

8How to Read / Guardrails

The most important filter: Reversion vs. Ruin. An oversold stock is not automatically a buy. The question is: why did it fall? If the answer is a temporary sentiment extreme over an intact business, that's a reversion candidate. If the answer is a structural break (fraud, bankruptcy, going-concern, failed drug trial, debt blowup), an oversold reading is a warning — not an entry signal. Every name in Section 2 has been checked against this filter.
Falling Knife Test
Reject names down 50%+ in days with no stabilization. Reject buyout-repriced names. Reject companies with accounting issues or going-concern doubt.
Proxy / Equity Beta Flag
GDX, OIH, XOP, URA, COPX, MOO, SIL = equity ETFs. They carry company-specific risk on top of commodity price. A gold miner can fall even if gold is flat (operational issues, hedging, costs).
K-1 Tax Warning
USO, UNG, DBC, UCO, BOIL, CPER, most CurrencyShares = Schedule K-1 at tax time (complex partnership tax form). Prefer No-K-1 alternatives: PDBC, BCI, COMB for commodity exposure.
Leveraged / Futures ETF Decay
UCO, SCO, BOIL, KOLD, TQQQ, TBT (2x/3x) are for short-term tactical use only. Volatility decay + contango roll means they are NOT suitable for multi-week holds. For longer holds, prefer the unlevered fund (XLE vs UCO; TLT vs TMF).
Long-Vol Special Rule
VXX, UVXY, UVIX = NEVER buy as "oversold." Structural contango decay grinds them lower every day. Only trade them when stretched UP (fade the spike). A low RSI on VXX = normal drift, not a setup.
Earnings / Binary Events
Any name with earnings ⚠ within ~30 days carries binary risk that overrides the technical setup. The stock can gap 10–20% on earnings in either direction — a mean-reversion trade can become a losing earnings trade. Reduce size or exit before the number.
