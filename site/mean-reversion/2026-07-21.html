7Options Structures Legend

This legend explains the generic educational structures referenced in the tables above. Always verify data, confirm with your brokerage, and paper-trade before risking real money.

RSI (0–100): Momentum gauge — readings below 30 = possible oversold (selling may be exhausted); above 70 = possible overbought (buying may be exhausted). Not a standalone signal.
Bollinger %B (0–1+): Where price sits in its 20-day band — below 0 = outside lower band (stretched low); above 1 = outside upper band (stretched high).
Z-score: Standard deviations from the 50-day mean — above ±2 is statistically unusual and a candidate for mean reversion.
Bull Put Spread: Sell a higher put, buy a lower put (same expiry). Profits if price stays ABOVE the sold strike. Max loss = spread width. Favored for oversold setups with elevated IV.
Bull Call Spread: Buy a lower call, sell a higher call. Profits if price rises past lower strike. Defined max cost. Favored when IV is low (calls are cheaper).
Call Credit Spread: Sell a lower call, buy a higher call. Profits if price stays BELOW the sold strike. Max loss = spread width. Favored for overbought setups with elevated IV.
Bear Put Spread: Buy a higher put, sell a lower put. Profits on downside move. Defined cost. For overbought setups when IV is lower (puts are affordable).
Cash-Secured Put: Sell a put, hold enough cash to buy shares if assigned. Profits if price stays above strike. Generates premium. Only on stocks you'd be comfortable owning.
IV Elevated = Sell Premium: When implied volatility is high, options are expensive. Favor selling spreads (collect premium). When IV is low, options are cheap — favor buying spreads.
IV Crush: After an earnings announcement, IV often drops sharply. Long premium buyers lose value even if price moves correctly. Sell premium into earnings; buy premium after IV settles.
50-day SMA: The average closing price over the last 50 trading days — a common short-to-medium term "mean" for reversion analysis.
Death Cross: 50-day moving average crosses below the 200-day — a technical bearish signal suggesting medium-term trend is down.

8How to Read This Report / Guardrails

The Reversion-vs-Ruin Filter: A low RSI is necessary but NOT sufficient for a reversion trade. Always ask: Is this a sentiment flush over a sound business, or a permanent break? Reject names driven by fraud, going-concern doubt, failed drug trials, bankruptcy, or structural demand collapse — those are falling knives, not mean-reversion candidates. Only the names where the business appears intact and the move looks like positioning excess appear on these lists.

Special handling — Volatility ETPs (VXX, UVXY, SVXY): Long-vol ETPs (VXX, UVXY) ONLY work as fades when stretched UP on a spike. Their structural roll decay (futures contango) grinds them lower over time — a low RSI is drift, not a setup. NEVER buy them as an "oversold" recovery. Inverse-vol (SVXY, SVIX): opposite drift, opposite rules. Fading their up-moves as mean reversion is usually wrong.

Leveraged ETFs (UCO, BOIL, TQQQ, TBT, 2x/3x anything): Volatility decay and futures roll erode returns over time — use only for short-term tactical reversion, never multi-week holds. Prefer the unlevered equivalent (XLE vs UCO; SOXX vs SOXL) for holds beyond a few sessions.

K-1 Tax Flag: USO, UNG, UCO, DBC, BOIL issue a Schedule K-1 tax form — complex and late-arriving. For oil exposure with simpler taxes, prefer XLE/XOP (standard 1099). PDBC and BCI are "no K-1" commodity alternatives.

Proxy vs Spot: GDX/GDXJ track gold-mining companies, not gold spot. URA tracks uranium companies. OIH tracks oilfield services companies. These carry equity beta and can diverge significantly from the underlying commodity — they are not substitutes for direct commodity exposure.

ETF liquidity check: Always verify that an ETF's options chain has open interest and reasonable bid/ask spreads before entering. Tier C ETFs (GDXJ, SIL, UNG, BOIL, FXE, FXY) have thinner options markets — check your brokerage before placing any order.

Earnings dates are the #1 risk: Any name with a known binary event (earnings, FDA date, major guidance call) in the next 30 days can completely override the technical setup. Always verify the earnings calendar at your brokerage before entering a position. HAL reports TODAY (July 21). GM, IBM, Tesla report this week. Verify all others independently.
