7How to Read This Report

A quick reference for the scoring system and the terms used throughout. Results and their plain-English meaning are in the body above; this section covers the generic methodology.

Direction Score (−100 to +100)
A weighted composite: 30% ETF trend/structure, 25% relative strength vs SPY, 20% macro tailwind/headwind, 15% news/catalyst flow, 10% momentum/breadth. Zero = perfectly neutral. +100 = maximum bullish conviction; −100 = maximum bearish.
Conviction: High / Medium / Low
How clearly the evidence points one way. Low = evidence is mixed or thin. High = multiple independent signals align. Low conviction → smaller position sizing.
Swing-Conviction Score (0–100)
How strongly positioned a STOCK is to move hardest in its sector's direction over the swing horizon (3 days–6 weeks). Technically-led: 35% setup quality, 25% relative strength, 20% catalyst, 20% move-strength potential. Higher = more likely to be the biggest mover, not necessarily the "safest."
Fundamental Health (0–100)
