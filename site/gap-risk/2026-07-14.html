Blockade Reboots, CPI and Warsh Land on Same Morning — the Most Consequential Tuesday of the Summer

Markets opened the week on the back foot after President Trump announced late Sunday the U.S. would reimpose a naval blockade of Iranian shipping and levy a 20% toll on all cargo through the Strait of Hormuz, sending Brent crude up 9.6% in a single session — its biggest one-day gain since May 2020. The NDX fell 1.88%, the SPX shed 0.79%, small-caps (RUT) lost 0.83%, and the DJX dipped 0.26% as the risk-off shock was most acute in rate-sensitive, high-multiple tech. Overnight Tuesday (Asia/early Europe) oil is extending gains another ~2%, blockade enforcement officially begins at 4 p.m. ET today, and two live grenades drop before noon: June CPI at 8:30 a.m. ET (consensus: headline −0.1% m/m, ~3.9% y/y, core 2.9% y/y) and Fed Chair Warsh’s debut Humphrey-Hawkins testimony to the House Financial Services Committee at 10 a.m. ET. Futures overnight are little changed, suggesting the tape is in a tense holding pattern ahead of those prints — the final lean tilts 63/54/58/50 after the engine folds in the gamma and catalyst reads below.

  • DJX IS THE ANCHOR: Dow held best on Monday (−0.26%), sits above both MAs, VXD 13.96 is the calmest vol read in the pack, and bank earnings could provide a modest offset.
  • FUTURES STABLE — FOR NOW: S&P 500 futures little changed pre-dawn; any CPI or Warsh surprise will move these quickly.
