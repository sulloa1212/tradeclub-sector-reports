Conviction: HIGH
+78
BULLISH
Thesis (plain English): Oil prices are spiking because the Strait of Hormuz — the world's most important oil shipping lane, carrying ~20% of global supply — has effectively re-closed due to renewed US–Iran fighting. Brent crude hit ~$87 today, up ~14% in two sessions. Upstream producers and refiners earn more when oil is expensive, and US Gulf Coast refiners in particular face minimal supply disruption while benefiting from inflated crack spreads.

Supporting points:
  • XLE is up +21% YTD, second only to XLK among GICS sectors — trend firmly intact.
  • Hormuz traffic down 50%+ this week; Goldman estimates only ~45% of regional exports will be insulated by pipeline bypasses by 2027, so disruption risk is real and sustained.
  • US producers (Permian, Gulf Coast) are the swing beneficiaries — not exposed to strait risk, but priced at higher Brent benchmarks.
  • Energy inflation also makes it harder for the Fed to cut — removing a key risk that could derail the sector.
Biggest risk: A surprise ceasefire or MoU re-signing between the US and Iran would immediately collapse the oil premium. Goldman's base-case Brent target remains $76, implying the spike is partly geopolitical premium.
