7Options Structures Legend

This legend explains the general method — the specific results and "why this read" are in the tables above. Always confirm any structure with your broker's risk tools and paper-trade before going live.

Direction + IV → Defined-Risk Structure Map

📈 Oversold + High IV (most common today)

Cash-Secured Put: Sell an OTM put; keep cash to buy shares if assigned. Collects premium; you want the stock to stay above the strike.

Bull Put Spread: Sell a put, buy a lower-strike put for protection. Defined risk, defined reward. Ideal when IV is high and you believe the stock stabilizes.

Bull Call Spread: Buy a call, sell a higher-strike call. Lower cost than buying a naked call. Use when you expect a bounce but IV is too high to buy calls outright.

📉 Overbought + High IV (common in energy/metals today)

Bear Call Spread: Sell a call, buy a higher-strike call. Profits if stock stays below your sold strike. Defined risk — the bought call caps your loss.

Bear Put Spread: Buy a put, sell a lower-strike put. Directional bet on decline. Lower cost than naked put. Use when IV is moderate (high IV makes bought puts expensive).

📖 Indicator Glossary
RSI (14) — a 0–100 momentum gauge; below 30 = oversold, above 70 = overbought, below 20 / above 80 = extreme.
Bollinger %B — measures where price sits relative to its band; above 1 = outside upper band (overbought); below 0 = outside lower band (oversold).
Z-score — how many standard deviations price is from its mean; above +2 or below −2 = statistically unusual.
ATR (Average True Range) — average daily price swing; a "big move" is 2–3× ATR in one direction.
IV / Implied Volatility — the market's estimate of future price movement priced into options; high IV = more expensive options. Mean-reversion + high IV → sell premium. Mean-reversion + low IV → buy options outright.
50-day SMA — the average closing price over the last 50 trading days; a common "mean" reference for medium-term reversion.
